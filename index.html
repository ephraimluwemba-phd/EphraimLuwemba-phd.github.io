Bio

I am currently enrolled as a PhD student at the Horizon Centre for Doctoral Training (University of Nottingham) where my research focuses on assessing guidelines and standards for age-appropriate design on the web. You can find my Horizon bio here.

Through the Horizon program, I am partnered with 5Rights, a charity created by Baroness Beeban Kidron with the mission to articulate the rights of children in the digital environment. I am due to start an internship with 5Rights during the summer of 2020, where I will learn about how their organisation operates, and how policy work is achieved in the real world. Following my time with them I plan on conducting some practice-informed (and eventually, practice-informing), research on age-appropriate design and how it can be implemented in practice.

I have a background in politics and philosophy (joint honours UG, and a postgraduate research degree in philosophy). My academic focuses prior to starting my PhD were in the areas of personal identity and in the political theory of the public realm (particularly Hannah Arendt). Following my masters I worked in the digital advertising industry where I gained an appreciation for the complex ethical challenges created by the web and how this can be a threat to our society's development. My experiences in marketing met with my background in politics, leading me to where I am today.
